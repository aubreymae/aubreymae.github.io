DMByte

As Digital Media students at York University, my group and I understood the shared difficulty of accessible academic advising. Long waitlists meant our urgent questions couldn't get answered immediately. As part of my Capstone project, my group and I created a mobile chatbot that acts as an academic advisor, ready to answer Digital Media-related questions by students.

My role

I was in charge of systems programming and using Android Studio, I created the logic system of the chatbot and it's database. Google's Dialogflow was used to support natural language processing.

How it works

The app works to personalize each student's progress. A logic system takes the student's name and a password (if new to the app) and then creates a database of their student record. The app works through The Set-Up Phase or the Tutorial first where the bot asks what courses the student has taken, if any. After this phase, the student is free to ask the chatbot any sorts of questions they wish.

Takeaways

This was an extremely ambitious project because my group and I had no prior experience developing on mobile, databases, nor natural language processing.

NLP was the biggest hurdle despite the wonderful Dialogflow. We found that even with Dialogflow's complex NLP system, we couldn't use it to a more personalized level. When the student asked for something specific (ie. what course is available based on GPA) Dialogflow had no way of connecting with the phone's own database.

Firebase was our cloud-based solution, but due to time constraints, we were not able to flesh out this feature.

DMByte was an incredible learning experience, especially with working backend. Despite the obstacles, we presented a polished Set-Up phase and some of the free-chat feature.
