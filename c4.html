Catalysing Community through Creative Coding at Firgrove

Working with Professor Joel Ong (Department of Computational Arts, AMPD, York University) and David Han and the Firgrove Learning and Innovation Center, AR/VR workshops were held to empower the youth of the Firgrove Crescent Community.

Inspired by the transitional neighbourhood of the Jane-Finch area and the various community and landscape changes that each resident have and are currently experiencing, the project provides students an artistic tool to express and explore their neighborhood.

The project aims to provide instruction to students on how to create computational art at the most basic level through the CoSpaces AR/VR platform using the Ricoh 360 video cameras, HTC Vive, and Zoom recorders.

My role

I worked as a Research Assistant responsible for researching equipment and platform use, preparing assets like instructional handouts and exemplary virtual worlds on CoSpaces, and hosting the workshops on site.

I offered instruction on equipment use (Ricoh Theta, HTC Vive, and Zoom recorders) and how to develop a virtual world using the CoSpaces plataform.
